PGP Harvard

The Harvard Personal Genome Project was initiated by George Church in 2005. As the first site in the global network, it has been devoted to prototyping and evaluating the open consent framework and related research practices. It began with a pilot study of ten fully identified individuals, known as the “PGP-10” and has incrementally scaled-up the cohort to thousands of enrolled volunteers.
